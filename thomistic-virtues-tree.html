A. Theological Virtues

Primary ST refs: I–II (theological virtues generally), II–II, q.1–3 for definitions and acts.

Faith — definition, parts, acts, vices, Scripture
Thomistic definition: supernatural habit of assent to revealed truth on God's authority. (ST II–II, q.1)

Parts / Acts: assent (believing), confessing, teaching, meditating; acts include believing articles, professing, perseverance in belief.

Modes / Species: implicit vs. explicit faith; degrees of certitude.

Opposed vices: infidelity, heresy, apostasy, incredulity.

Scripture anchors: Heb 11:1; Rom 10:9–10; Jn 6:29; Mt 17:20.

Hope — definition, parts, acts, vices, Scripture
Thomistic definition: supernatural desire & expectation of eternal life and means thereto, relying on God's promises. (ST II–II, q.17)

Parts / Acts: trust, confident expectation, prayer, perseverance.

Opposed vices: despair (defect), presumption (excess).

Scripture anchors: Rom 8:24–25; Heb 6:19; Ps 42:11; Is 40:31.

Charity (Love) — definition, parts, acts, vices, Scripture
Thomistic definition: infused love by which we love God above all and neighbor for God's sake. (ST II–II, q.23–26)

Parts / Acts: love of God; love of neighbor — mercy, almsgiving, forgiveness, fraternal correction, joy in another's good, suffering for others.

Modes / Species: friendship of charity; paternal charity; fraternal charity.

Opposed vices: hatred (of God or neighbor), spiritual apathy/sloth, envy, malice.

Scripture anchors: 1 Cor 13; Jn 13:34–35; Mt 22:37–40; 1 Jn 4:7–8.

B. The Four Cardinal Virtues (expanded)

Primary Thomistic treatments in ST II–II, q.47–189. Each cardinal virtue has integral parts, species (modes), potential parts, acts, and opposed vices.

1. Prudence (II–II, q.47–56) — Right reason applied to action
Definition: practical intellect ordering means to ends; "right reason applied to action."
Integral parts (Aquinas)
  • Memory — recall of past particulars to inform judgment; acts: recalling analogues when deliberating.
  • Understanding — grasping circumstances & first principles.
  • Docility — openness to instruction, counsel.
  • Shrewdness — quickness in detecting means/ends.
  • Reasoning — ordered deliberation.
  • Foresight (provisio) — planning for future contingencies.
  • Circumspection — considering circumstances before acting.
  • Caution — avoiding probable evils.

Scripture anchors: Prov 2–3; Prov 11:14; Lk 14:28–33; Mt 10:16.

Subjective species (kinds of prudence)
  • Personal/Individual prudence — prudence for daily personal choices.
  • Domestic prudence — ordering a household's goods and morals.
  • Political prudence — prudence in public office and laws.
  • Military prudence — prudence applied to warfare & defense.
Potential parts & related notions
  • Euboulia — good counsel; acts: advising others prudently.
  • Synesis — sound ordinary judgment about common matters.
  • Gnome — judgment about extraordinary or rare matters.

Typical acts: deliberating, choosing means, issuing counsel, counting cost. Opposed vices: rashness, misplaced timidity, malice in counsel.

2. Justice (II–II, q.57–122) — Rendering to each his due
Definition: habit by which one gives each his due; ordered to the common good. Divided classically into commutative, distributive, legal.
Major divisions (species)
  • Commutative Justice — private exchanges, contracts, restitution. Acts: honest trade, repayment, restitution. Opposed vices: fraud, theft, usury. Scripture: Lev 19:13; Lk 19:8.
  • Distributive Justice — proportionate allocation of honors, goods by community. Acts: fair wages, appointments. Opposed vices: partiality, nepotism. Scripture: Acts 6:1–6.
  • Legal Justice — duties owed to the common good (taxes, civic obedience). Acts: paying taxes, obedience to legitimate authority. Scripture: Rom 13:1–7; Mt 22:21.
Potential parts — justice-related virtues (Aquinas lists many)
  • Religion — justice toward God: worship, prayer, vows, sacrifices, tithes. Opposed vices: sacrilege, irreverence. (II–II, q.81–83)
  • Piety — duties to parents & country: honoring parents, civic duty. Opposed vices: contempt, ingratitude. (II–II, q.101)
  • Obedience — submission to legitimate superiors. (II–II, q.101)
  • Gratitude — repaying benefits, thanksgiving. (II–II, q.103)
  • Truthfulness / Veracity — honest speech; opposed: lying, flattery, detraction. (II–II, q.109–113)
  • Liberality (Generosity) — right giving & spending; opposed: avarice, prodigality. (II–II, q.117–122)
  • Affability — courteous fellowship, mildness in speech. (II–II, q.108)
  • Equity (Epieikeia) — correcting the legal letter when justice requires. (II–II, q.120)
  • Vindication — establishing rights & restitution (private vengeance restrained). (II–II, q.122)

Representative Scripture: Micah 6:8; Mt 22:21; Lev 19:15–18; Rom 13:1–7.

Typical acts: fulfilling contracts, paying debts, giving due honors. Opposed vices: fraud, injustice, theft, partiality.

3. Fortitude (II–II, q.123–140) — firmness in difficulties
Definition: firmness in difficulties; moderates fear and fosters daring for the good.
Principal acts
  • Endurance (sustinere) — patient bearing of trials; acts: patience in sufferings.
  • Aggression / Daring (aggredi) — courageous action to remove obstacles to good.
Species / modes
  • Magnanimity — greatness of soul re: honors and high tasks; acts: undertaking great works, dignified endurance of insult. Opposed: pusillanimity (defect), vanity (excess). (II–II, q.129–133)
  • Magnificence — fitting largeness in expenditure for great works; acts: funding temples, public works; opposed: parsimony, prodigality. (II–II, q.134–136)
Potential parts / related virtues
  • Patience — bearing evil without complaint.
  • Perseverance — constancy in good over time.

Scripture anchors: Jas 1:12; 1 Pet 5:8–9; Ps 27:14; Is 41:10.

4. Temperance (II–II, q.141–170) — moderation of pleasures
Definition: moderation regarding pleasurable goods; ordering sensual appetite to reason & God.
Integral parts (Aquinas)
  • Verecundia — shamefacedness; a reverent reserve in conduct and demeanor.
  • Honesty (decorum / love of spiritual beauty) — proper exterior demeanor reflecting inner moderation.
Subjective species (major kinds)
  • Abstinence — restraint regarding food and drink (gluttony). (II–II, q.148)
  • Sobriety — restraint from drunkenness. (II–II, q.149)
  • Chastity — right ordering of sexual appetite; species: continence (interior mastery), virginity (special mode), fidelity in marriage. (II–II, q.151–154)
  • Modesty — decorum in dress, speech, and behavior (1 Tim 2:9). (II–II, q.161)
  • Studiosity — measured inquiry; avoiding immoderate curiosity. (II–II, q.156)
  • Eutrapelia (pleasantness / proper play) — balanced recreation between boorishness and buffoonery. (II–II, q.167)
Potential parts (virtues closely related)
  • Continence — interior mastery over passions.
  • Meekness — gentleness of spirit.
  • Clemency — moderation in punishing.

Representative Scripture: Gal 5:22–23; 1 Cor 6:18–20; Mt 5:27–30; Phil 4:5.

C. Intellectual Virtues

Speculative & Practical intellect virtues with Thomistic notes. Primary references in ST I–II and operative in practical prudence.

Speculative Intellect Virtues
  • Understanding (Intellectus) — immediate grasp of first principles; acts: recognition of truths. (Scripture: Prov 4:7)
  • Science (Scientia) — knowledge of demonstrated truths; acts: teaching, explaining. (Scripture: Col 2:3)
  • Wisdom (Sapientia) — highest contemplative grasp ordered to God (also Gift of the Spirit). (Scripture: Wis 7:7–12; Jn 17:3)
Practical Intellect Virtues

Prudence is the chief practical intellect virtue (see B.1 above). Its integral parts realize the practical intellect in action.

E. Gifts of the Holy Spirit

How gifts perfect virtues — ST I–II, q.68; gifts derive language from Isaiah 11:2–3 as read in tradition.

List & function
  • Wisdom — perfects charity in contemplation of divine things.
  • Understanding — helps grasp divine mysteries (perfects faith/knowledge).
  • Knowledge — infused ability to judge created things in relation to God.
  • Counsel — immediate guidance in concrete decisions (perfects prudence).
  • Fortitude (gift) — steadies the will in trials beyond natural strength.
  • Piety — disposes to worship God in right love (perfects religion/piety).
  • Fear of the Lord — reverential fear ordering the soul away from offending God (perfects temperance / reverence).

Scripture anchor: Isa 11:2–3; 1 Cor 12 (context of spiritual gifts).

F. Beatitudes & Fruits of the Spirit

Beatitudes: Mt 5:3–10 — Aquinas maps each to a virtue; Fruits: Gal 5:22–23 — effects of charity.

Beatitudes (Matthew 5:3–10)
  • Poor in spirit → humility (Temperance/Charity)
  • Mourn → contrition (Prudence & Charity)
  • Meek → meekness (Temperance)
  • Hunger & thirst for righteousness → justice
  • Merciful → charity (mercy)
  • Pure in heart → chastity
  • Peacemakers → charity
  • Persecuted for righteousness → fortitude

Scripture: Mt 5:3–10

Fruits of the Spirit (Gal 5:22–23)
  • Charity, Joy, Peace, Patience, Benignity, Goodness, Longanimity, Mildness, Faithfulness, Modesty, Continency, Chastity

These are habitual effects flowing from infused charity; each has corresponding acts (e.g., mercy is an act of charity; patience is an act of fortitude & charity).

Scripture: Gal 5:22–23

G. States of Life / Special Modes

Aquinas treats certain states (virginity, marriage, widowed, religious profession) as special modes that perfect certain virtues or order acts differently.

Examples & moral significance
  • Virginity — a special mode of chastity when perpetual continence is practised; acts: consecration, perpetual continence. (Scripture: Mt 19:12; 1 Cor 7)
  • Marriage — ordered to children & mutual help; works of justice, charity, temperance evaluated in that state (Scripture: Eph 5:22–33).
  • Widowhood and Religious Profession — states that order particular acts of charity and chastity in distinct ways.

Chastity — species: continence (interior mastery), virginity (special mode), marital fidelity. Acts: guarding senses, ordered speech, proper marital acts. Opposed vices: fornication, adultery, lust. (1 Cor 6:18–20; Mt 5:27–30)

Magnanimity — greatness of soul; acts: willing great tasks, enduring insult; opposed: pusillanimity, vanity. (1 Cor 9:24–27)

Magnificence — fitting largeness in expenditure; acts: funding worthy public works; opposed: parsimony, prodigality. (1 Chron 29)

Charity → Works of Mercy (expanded)

Charity's concrete acts: corporal & spiritual works of mercy — feeding, sheltering, instructing, admonishing. Aquinas treats these as direct expressions of charity. (Mt 25:31–46; Lk 10:25–37)
